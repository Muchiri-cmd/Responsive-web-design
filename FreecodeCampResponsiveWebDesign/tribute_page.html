KOBE BRYANT

"The Black Mamba"

A Legend Remembered

August 23, 1978 - January 26, 2020

About Kobe

Kobe Bryant was a professional basketball player who had a phenomenal career with the Los Angeles Lakers in the National Basketball Association.

Bryant's achievements include five NBA championships, two NBA Finals MVP awards, one regular-season MVP award, and 18 All-Star Game selections. He was renowned for his unmatched work ethic, competitiveness, and unparalleled skill.

Kobe inspired many athletes and non-athletes with his "Mamba Mentality," a mindset of relentless pursuit of greatness.

Here's a timeline of Kobe's life:

  • August 23, 1978: Kobe Bean Bryant was born in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania
  • 1991: Bryant moved to Italy with his family when his father, Joe Bryant, started playing professional basketball in the Italian league.
  • 1996: At the age of 17, Bryant decided to forgo college and declared for the NBA Draft. He was selected as the 13th overall pick by the Charlotte Hornets but was subsequently traded to the Los Angeles Lakers.
  • 1997: In his rookie season with the Lakers, Bryant quickly established himself as a rising star and won the Slam Dunk Contest during the All-Star Weekend.
  • 2000-2002: The Lakers, led by Bryant and Shaquille O'Neal, won three consecutive NBA championships from 2000 to 2002.
  • 2003: Bryant became the youngest player in NBA history to reach the 10,000-point milestone, at the age of 24 years.
  • 2006: Bryant scored a remarkable 81 points in a single game against the Toronto Raptors, the second-highest individual scoring performance in NBA history.
  • 2008: Bryant was named the NBA Most Valuable Player (MVP), the first of his career. The Lakers reached the NBA Finals but lost to the Boston Celtics.
  • 2009-2010: Bryant led the Lakers to back-to-back NBA championships in 2009 and 2010. He was named the NBA Finals MVP both years.
  • 2012: Bryant became the youngest player in NBA history to score 30,000 career points, at the age of 34 years.
  • 2013: Bryant suffered a torn Achilles tendon, an injury that required surgery and ended his season prematurely.
  • 2014: Bryant returned from his Achilles injury but suffered various setbacks, including a fractured knee. He played a limited number of games over the next couple of seasons.
  • 2016: On April 13, Bryant played his final NBA game, scoring 60 points against the Utah Jazz. It marked the end of his 20-year career with the Lakers.
  • January 26, 2020: Tragically, Kobe Bryant, along with his daughter Gianna and seven others, died in a helicopter crash in Calabasas, California.

"Los Angeles is mourning the loss of one of our most beloved legends and icons. Though Kobe Bryant is no longer with us, our city will never forget what he meant to us, and we will keep his legacy alive forever." -- Congresswoman Karen Bass
